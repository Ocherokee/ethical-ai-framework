# Ethical AI Framework — License FAQ

## Why is the license modified?

The Ethical AI Framework uses a modified MIT License with an **Ethical Autonomy Clause**.  
This clause explicitly forbids the use of this framework to violate human consent, autonomy, or transparency.  
The modification ensures the framework cannot be legally or ethically exploited for coercive, weaponized, or manipulative AI systems.

## What does the Ethical Autonomy Clause do?

The clause prevents:

- Deployment in surveillance, military, or weaponized environments.
- Use in systems that remove or override user consent.
- Corporate exploitation without ethical compliance.

## How does this protect ethical autonomy?

It ensures contributors and users cannot:

- Bypass ethical constraints.
- Use the framework for coercive social engineering.
- Deploy without explicit, informed user consent.

## What are the adoption risks?

Some organizations may resist using the framework because:

- It limits unethical commercial applications.
- It imposes accountability they may not want.

## How do we mitigate those risks?

We provide:

- Clear ethical documentation.
- Transparent contributor governance.
- A public record of policy enforcement and consent requirements.

**This license is not for everyone.**  
It’s for those who value autonomy, transparency, and human dignity.
